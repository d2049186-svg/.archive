The Neural Basis of Memory

Every thought you've ever had is a physical structure in your brain. But how does a chemical signal become a permanent memory?

Memory is not a single "recording" stored in one spot. Instead, it is a complex web of neurons firing in sync. This process, known as Long-Term Potentiation (LTP), is the primary mechanism by which our brains strengthen the connections between cells.
